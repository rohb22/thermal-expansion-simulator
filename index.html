About Thermal Expansion

Thermal expansion is the tendency of matter to change its shape, area, and volume in response to a change in temperature. When a substance is heated, its particles begin to move more quickly, maintaining a greater average separation, causing the substance to expand.

This simulator helps visualize how different materials expand or contract under varying temperature conditions, making it an excellent tool for understanding this fundamental physics concept.

How to Use the Simulator

  1. Select a material from the dropdown menu
  2. Enter the initial length of your material
  3. Input the initial temperature in °C
  4. Set the final temperature in °C
  5. Click "Simulate" to see the expansion effect
  6. Use the "Reset" button to start over
